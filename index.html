About the practice

Two-person environmental science studio
Massachusetts · Field + geospatial

Complementary strengths.
One coordinated scope.

Common Ground is being built by two environmental scientists who share a commitment to restoring native habitats and making ecological evidence genuinely useful.

One side of the practice focuses on coastal systems, birds, native habitat, biogeochemistry, and restoration monitoring. The other brings biodiversity assessment, coastal and wetland ecology, GIS, and remote sensing. Together, we connect field conditions, landscape context, and practical stewardship.

Ecological Baseline + Stewardship Plan

Geospatial capability

Habitat Change + Connectivity Analysis

  • Support stewardship and land-protection strategy
  • Document methods, confidence, and limitations

03

Restoration planning

Coastal + Native Habitat Restoration Screen

Existing-condition review, restoration opportunity zones, bird and biodiversity considerations, management constraints, and a phased concept for the next level of work.

  • Shape native habitat priorities
  • Define the specialists and information needed next

01
Portfolio study · Site partner search

Biodiversity baseline & stewardship priorities

Public or permission-based conservation parcel · Massachusetts

Client decision

Which habitat units and stewardship issues should receive attention over the next one to three years?

Evidence

Seasonal field observations, fixed photo points, habitat stratification, imagery, wetlands, hydrography, and conservation context.

Decision output

Habitat-condition scorecard, priority management zones, one-year action plan, and a repeatable monitoring protocol.

Follow-on scope

Seasonal monitoring, invasive-species planning, restoration concepts, volunteer training, or grant support.

Habitat change & connectivity screen

Conservation network study area · Massachusetts

Client decision

Where has habitat cover apparently changed, and which areas deserve field verification or stewardship attention first?

Evidence

Comparable-date aerial and Sentinel-2 imagery, broad land cover, protected lands, roads, wetlands, and transparent landscape metrics.

Follow-on scope

Targeted field checks, corridor planning, annual imagery updates, landowner coordination, or restoration screening.
