Our point of view

Most organizations aren’t data-poor.
They’re decision-poor.

Public layers, volunteer knowledge, old reports, and field notes already hold part of the story. The missing piece is often the time and specialist capacity to turn them into defensible priorities.

We combine field ecology, GIS, remote sensing, and monitoring design in one coordinated scope—then show what to do first, what to verify, and what can wait.

Conor McDonnell is a Massachusetts-based environmental scientist whose experience spans freshwater monitoring, fisheries data collection, forest pest inventories, aquatic vegetation management, GIS mapping, and laboratory ecotoxicology.

Across public agencies, consulting firms, and research settings, he has learned how ecological evidence becomes useful: collect it consistently, state its limits clearly, and connect it to a decision someone can act on.

Our portfolio work follows the same standard as a client assignment: a bounded question, defensible methods, explicit limitations, practical recommendations, and a clear scope for what comes next.

01
Pilot engagement

Biodiversity baseline & stewardship priorities

Public or permission-based conservation parcel · Massachusetts

Client decision

Which habitat units and stewardship issues should receive attention over the next one to three years?

Evidence

Seasonal field observations, fixed photo points, habitat stratification, imagery, wetlands, hydrography, and conservation context.

Decision output

Habitat-condition scorecard, priority management zones, one-year action plan, and a repeatable monitoring protocol.

Follow-on scope

Seasonal monitoring, invasive-species planning, restoration concepts, volunteer training, or grant support.

02
Demonstration scope

Habitat change & connectivity screen

Conservation network study area · Massachusetts

Client decision

Where has habitat cover apparently changed, and which areas deserve field verification or stewardship attention first?

Evidence

Comparable-date aerial and Sentinel-2 imagery, broad land cover, protected lands, roads, wetlands, and transparent landscape metrics.

Decision output

Confidence-coded change areas, connectivity indicators, candidate pinch points, and a field-validation priority map.

Follow-on scope

Targeted field checks, corridor planning, annual imagery updates, landowner coordination, or restoration screening.
